Who I Am

An avid outdoorsman, animal-lover, and beer connoisseur, I am a laid-back fullstack web developer who enjoys solving problems and helping others.

I started with web design in 1998 as a result of a science project, and have enjoyed it as a hobby ever since. Over the years I added CSS and Javascript to made more visually engaging and interactive websites, but it still remained a hobby as I hopped from major to major in college trying to find "my passion." I guess I thought playing around with HTML, CSS, and Javascript was "too fun" to be considered a job, but, thankfully, after a friend opened my eyes to the world of web development, I knew I had found my calling. Having previously worked purely on front-end content, it was mindblowing to see what was achievable when paired with a custom back-end.

From this point onward, my goal is to use all that I've learned (and all I have yet to learn) to provide knowledge, entertainment, and solutions to people I may never meet in person, but whose lives will be improved nonetheless.

Latchkeyperclose

An app designed to make the lives of working parents easier as well as make chores more fun for school-age "latchkey" kids. The intent is to have an easy way for parents to assign and check on chores without having to constantly nag, as well as incentivize kids to do chores by allowing children to suggest, and parents to approve, a reward system.

This app feature's an adult's page and a children's page. The parent's page features a "message from kids" area, an editable roster of children with their current standing "chore point" total, a table displaying chores already completed by their children, the suggested reward from each kid for parent approval and "point cost" assignment, and a chart showing chore completion by day to see if trends in completion develop. The children's page features a message area from parents, the list of chores to complete, and an area to recommend rewards for which they can redeem their "chore points."
